The Expedition of the Thousand

A historical GIS reconstruction of Garibaldi’s 1860 campaign from Quarto to Naples.

Overview

This project maps the route of the Expedition of the Thousand, led by Giuseppe Garibaldi in 1860, from Quarto near Genoa to Naples.

Interpretation

The map highlights a bottom-up strategy of unification. Instead of starting from the North with strong institutional support, Garibaldi began from the South with a small force and moved northward through local victories.

Mapping Choices

  • Blue dashed lines for sea routes
  • Solid red lines for land routes
  • Crossed swords for battle locations
  • House symbol for the starting point
  • Flag symbol for the final destination
  • Interactive popups with place, date and event description

Limitations

The main uncertainty concerns the exact routes between points, especially at sea. The goal of the map is not to reconstruct every precise trajectory, but to represent the sequence and logic of the campaign.

Tools

QGIS · historical mapping · geocoding · movement visualization
