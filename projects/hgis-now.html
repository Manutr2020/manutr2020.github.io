Mapping Italian Expressions for “Now”

An HGIS project on the spatial distribution of mo, ora and adesso in early twentieth-century Italian dialect data.

Overview

This project explores whether three Italian temporal adverbs meaning “now” simply mark lexical variation or reflect deeper cultural and identity boundaries across Italy.

Data

The project uses data from the Atlante Italo-Svizzero (AIS), based on fieldwork conducted between 1919 and 1935. The final dataset includes 369 points classified into lexical families.

Methods

  • Manual georeferencing of AIS maps in QGIS
  • Creation of point layers for documented locations
  • Classification into MO, ORA, ADESSO and OTHER families
  • Point map and Voronoi polygon map
  • Comparison with dialect and historical maps

Main Findings

The maps show a strong regional pattern: adesso dominates in the North, ora is prominent in Central Italy, and mo clearly prevails in the South and parts of Sardinia. The result suggests that the variation is not random, but connected to historically rooted linguistic systems.

Tools

QGIS · HGIS · Natural Earth · AIS · spatial interpretation
