That-Omission in English

A variationist sociolinguistic analysis of optional that omission using mixed-effects logistic regression.

Overview

This project investigates the alternation between overt that and omission in English complement clauses, such as “I think that she is right” versus “I think she is right”.

Research Question

How is that-omission conditioned by linguistic and social factors, while accounting for differences between individual speakers?

Methods

  • Replication of part of Tagliamonte’s variationist analysis
  • Mixed-effects logistic regression in R
  • Linguistic predictors: verb, subject type, tense, complexity
  • Social predictors: age, occupation, sex, education
  • Random intercepts for speaker variation

Main Findings

Linguistic factors play the strongest role. Verbs such as think and simple syntactic structures favor omission, while more complex structures reduce it. Social factors are weaker, with occupation showing some effect.

Tools

R · lme4 · ggplot2 · mixed-effects models · variationist sociolinguistics
